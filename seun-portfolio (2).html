Overview

FAM20 is an exclusive delivery benefit for GoPuff's paid subscription tier — FAM — guaranteeing delivery in under 20 minutes for $1.49/order. Half the cost of standard priority delivery, it was designed as both a retention hook for existing members and a top-of-funnel acquisition driver.

The experiment ran multiple test groups: one cohort received FAM20 for free, and separate tiered groups were offered FAM20 as a per-order add-on at different price points — testing both behavior change and willingness to pay simultaneously.

The experiment more than doubled delivery upgrade take rate — from 12% on priority to 25.5% on FAM20 — while lifting paid membership signups by 17% and driving 12% order growth among users who received FAM20 for free.

My Contributions

  • Served as one of two Lead PMs from concept through experimentation to nationwide launch.
  • Personally built the marketing landing page at gopuff.com/offer/fam20 — copy, structure, and FAQ design.
  • Designed a multi-group experiment with data scientists: a free FAM20 cohort to measure behavior change, plus separate groups offered FAM20 as a per-order add-on at tiered price points to measure willingness to pay and find the optimal take rate.
  • Delivered weekly executive reports to the CEOs, synthesizing cohort data across all test groups into go/no-go recommendations.
  • Scoped the money-back guarantee mechanic: orders arriving 10+ min late trigger an automatic $1.49 credit — building customer trust into the SLA.

Outcome

The experiment validated both demand and pricing. FAM20 more than doubled the delivery upgrade take rate (12% → 25.5%), drove a 17% lift in paid membership signups, and grew order frequency by 12% among the free cohort — proving the feature increased engagement, not just one-time conversion. Results earned full executive sponsorship and a nationwide launch.

Thrive

Problem

Minority college students — especially Black and Hispanic communities — face a critical gap in mental healthcare. 75% of mental health professionals in the US are white, leaving patients without counselors who understand their culture, language, or lived experiences. Only 1 in 5 Black, Asian, and Native American adults with mental illness actually receives treatment.

Research & Discovery

I led the product discovery process, combining desk research with primary user research. I reviewed NIMH statistics, ADAA reports, and existing platform reviews, then ran surveys with HBCU students to understand their actual barriers.

The key finding that reframed our entire strategy: cost and access ranked below identity and cultural fit as barriers. Students weren't avoiding care because they couldn't afford it — they were avoiding it because the platforms didn't feel built for them.

Competitive Analysis

I mapped the landscape across three axes: professional credibility, cultural relevance, and community. No existing product scored well on all three. Headspace had credibility but zero cultural specificity. Tbh had community but no clinical depth. That three-way gap was our opening.

Being seen is therapeutic in itself. We weren't building another wellness app — we were building the first mental health platform where minority students are the default, not the edge case.

Product Decisions

  • Defined two hard constraints as PM — Thrive is a culturally fluent guide; it is not a replacement for professional therapy. Every feature was evaluated against these.
  • Championed the AI coach concept — Emily (Empathy), Kobe (Kindness), Sara (Sympathy) — giving users identity-aligned connection from session one.
  • Shaped the onboarding flow to be fast and empathetic — sign-in felt like an invitation into a safe space, not a clinical intake form.
  • Prioritized a community layer alongside AI coaches — peer connection is as important as individual support for this demographic.

Pitch Strategy

Pitched solo to judges from Microsoft, Verizon, Innovate Alabama, and RCX Sports Foundation at the Alabama Collective × Innovate Alabama HBCU Business Pitch Competition during the Magic City Classic in Birmingham, AL.

Structured the pitch around three beats: the scale of the problem (NIMH data), the failure of existing solutions (competitive gap analysis), and why our team was uniquely positioned to solve it.

Outcome

First place and $15K in non-dilutive funding. Thrive was subsequently featured in Alabama A&M University's press, and the win opened conversations with Microsoft and Innovate Alabama as early partners — validating the product thesis with exactly the community we were building for.

Problem

As AI adoption accelerates, most companies deploying AI have no clear internal owner for safety and compliance. Regulatory frameworks — the EU AI Act, US executive orders, sector-specific guidelines — are complex, fast-moving, and inconsistently understood. Getting it wrong means regulatory exposure, reputational damage, and lost enterprise trust.

Solution

Ranti AI is an AI Safety & Compliance co-pilot: an intelligent, always-on layer that helps organizations track, understand, and act on AI governance requirements. Not a replacement for compliance teams — a force multiplier for the teams already at capacity.

I identified the market gap, built the pitch from scratch, secured $10K solo, and stepped in as PM to build the product from zero.

My Contributions

  • Identified the white space in AI compliance tooling and defined the positioning: co-pilot, not consultant, not portal.
  • Built and delivered the investor pitch solo, securing $10K in non-dilutive funding to seed early development.
  • Serving as PM: owning the roadmap, running stakeholder discovery, defining the core compliance workflow.
  • Translating the regulatory complexity of AI governance into a clear, buildable, adoptable SaaS product.

Status

Ranti AI is actively in development. Running structured discovery with legal, risk, and data teams at mid-market companies. Using findings to build a phased roadmap that prioritizes the highest-friction compliance tasks first.

Problem

Most companies have data but few people who can access it. Analytics tools require SQL, onboarding, and constant context-switching. Business stakeholders wait days for answers that should take seconds — because the data team is always the bottleneck.

Solution

DEX AI brings data analytics directly into Slack and Microsoft Teams. Upload a CSV, connect a database, or import from Google Sheets — then ask @DEX anything in plain English. Get charts, insights, and projections in the thread where your team already works. No dashboards. No SQL. No onboarding.

Three steps: upload → ask → get a chart. Under 60 seconds, right in Slack. That's the entire product promise — and the hardest constraint to hold.

My Contributions

  • Serving as PM, owning the full roadmap, feature prioritization, and go-to-market for Slack and Teams integrations.
  • Defined the core UX principle: zero-onboarding, three-step flow. Ruthlessly cut anything that added friction to the core loop.
  • Architected the four-tier pricing model (Free → $49 → $149 → $399/mo), balancing PLG adoption with enterprise monetization.
  • Shipped enterprise-grade features: role-based permissions, 256-bit encryption, audit trails, and connectors for PostgreSQL, MySQL, Google Sheets.
  • Driving waitlist strategy targeting sales ops, finance, and marketing analytics — roles where data questions are daily.

Status

DEX AI is live and accepting early access sign-ups. Iterating on the NLP query engine and expanding data source integrations. North star: make every team as data-fluent as their best analyst.

Thinking Process

"Your team is already in Slack. Why isn't your data?"

01 — OBSERVE

The Bottleneck

Non-technical stakeholders needed data constantly, but every path required a technical intermediary. The data team was always blocked.

02 — REFRAME

Channel Insight

The insight wasn't "build a better dashboard." It was: bring analytics to where decisions already happen — Slack threads, not separate portals.

03 — CONSTRAIN

Zero-Onboarding Rule

If it takes more than 3 steps to get a chart, we've failed. Forced ruthless prioritization of the core loop.

04 — MONETIZE

PLG Pricing

Free tier to drive adoption. Paid tiers to monetize team usage. Enterprise unlocks compliance features larger orgs need.

05 — GROW

GTM Focus

Targeting sales ops, finance, marketing analytics — roles where data questions are daily. DEX makes them self-sufficient.

Key PM Insight

The hardest product decision was what to leave out. The moment we added a "dashboard view," we became another BI tool. Staying Slack-native forced every feature to justify its place in the conversation thread. That constraint is the product.
